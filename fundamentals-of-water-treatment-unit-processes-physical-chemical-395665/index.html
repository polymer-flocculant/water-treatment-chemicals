Another physical process used in water treatment is filtration. Chemical Processes: Chemical processes involve the use of chemicals to remove impurities from water. These processes are mainly used to remove dissolved solids, such as minerals, metals, and organic compounds. One of the most commonly used chemical processes is coagulation, which is used to remove smaller particles that may not settle in sedimentation. Coagulants, such as alum, react with the particles in the water, causing them to clump together and settle. Another essential chemical process in water treatment is disinfection. This process involves the use of chemicals, such as chlorine or ozone, to kill harmful microorganisms in the water. Disinfection is crucial in ensuring that the water is safe for consumption and does not pose a health risk to humans. In addition to coagulation and disinfection, there are other chemical processes used in water treatment, such as ion exchange, reverse osmosis, and adsorption. Ion exchange involves the use of resins to remove ions, such as calcium and magnesium, from the water. Reverse osmosis uses a semi-permeable membrane to remove dissolved solids, while adsorption uses activated carbon to remove organic compounds. Importance of Proper Water Treatment: Proper water treatment is essential for several reasons. Firstly, it helps to ensure that the water is safe for consumption and does not pose a health risk to humans. Secondly, it helps to protect the environment by removing pollutants and contaminants from water sources. Water treatment also plays a crucial role in industries, where it is used for various purposes, such as cooling, cleaning, and manufacturing. In conclusion, water treatment unit processes, both physical and chemical, play a vital role in purifying water and making it safe for consumption and other purposes.
